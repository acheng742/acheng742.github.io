Work About Resume

Pet's Place is a US national pet supply delivery website. Pet's Place strives to deliver pet foods in a timely manner and offers a wide variety of brands to choose from based on the user's specific pet needs.

My Role

UX Designer designing an app for Pet's Place from conception to delivery.

Project Goal

Design a responsive web app for Pet's Place that allows users to easily food for their pets to be delivered.

Target Audience

People who have pets that lack the time or desire to leave the house to purchase their pet's food.

Challenge

Busy professionals and home-bound people lack the time or ability to shop at physical stores for their pet's needs.The goal is to create a product that provides a positive experience for those who prefer online shopping by including a variety of pet foods, supplies, toys, and more.

Research Study Details

I conducted interviews and created personas to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to shop in store. Rather than leaving the house, they prefer shopping online for those products. This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Pet's Place customers, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from leaving the house to shop in-person. Sometimes they are frustrated with their online shopping experience because there aren’t photos to accurately display the product, the website is unorganized and makes it difficult to browse, and some brands aren’t available or difficult to find.

Findings

- Some products don’t have photos, so they don’t know what to select. Having photos of each product makes for easy recognition.

- Cluttered layouts make it difficult to shop. Organized and uncluttered layout makes for easy browsing.

- Some particular brands are difficult to find. Prescription brands available for sensitive dogs is inclusive for all.

Initial Design Concepts

Usability Testing

KPIs

Time on task: How long does it take for a user to complete their order?

Drop-off rate: How often do users not complete and leave their order?

Conversion rate: How many users successfully places an order?

Tasks

- Ask user to select a pet food item to order.

- Ask user to add item to the cart and increase the quantity.

- Ask user to continue shopping for additional items.

- Ask user to complete the checkout process.

Results

- Users need a way to see when an item is added to their cart. Showing a popout when items are added provides assurance.

- Users need an option to populate their billing address after entering their shipping address to avoid redundancy.

- Users need a way to store their payment information​.
