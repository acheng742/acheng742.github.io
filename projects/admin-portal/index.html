Work About

The ITProTV Admin Portal (ProPortal) is logged-in administrator experience where IT leaders can manage their team's subscriptions, assign courses, and generate monthly usage reports.

My Role

UX Designer/Developer for a logged-in administrator managing experience from conception to implementation.

Project Goal

To provide an administrative user with tools to manage teams and view each team member's learning progress. Implementing visual representations of progress and growth will contribute to customer retention.

Target Audience

IT team leaders who are managing their employee's training journey.

Challenge

As a new product offering, the challenge was how critical information should be highlighted and accessed for a seamless use â€” deciding the best way to display the entire course library for the user to select courses and episodes they needed in the learning module creation wizard.

Research Study Details

The design team collected feedback from 30 IT team leaders through surveys and phone interviews. The study found that major contributors to their frustration were the inability to organize their employees into groups that focused on different learning paths and the lack of customized reporting options.

Findings

- Administrators need to be able to segment users into teams for reporting purposes.

- They need a way to create and assign learning modules to teams or individual users.

- They want to generate daily, monthly, or quarterly reports the team's or individual's usage.

Some Initial Design Concepts

Usability Testing

KPIs

Time on task: How long does it take for a user to create a team of 5 members?

Task success rate: How often do users successfully create a team?

Time on task: How long does it take for a user to assign a course to a team?

Task success rate: How often do users successfully assign a course to the team?

Time on task: How long does it take for a user generate a monthly report?

Task success rate: How often do users successfully generate a monthly report?

Time on task: How long does it take for a user to remove someone from a team?

Task success rate: How often do users successfully remove someone from a team?

Tasks

- Ask user to find the create a team consisting of 5 members.

- Ask user to create an assignment for the new team.

- Ask user to download and open a monthly report of usage.

- Ask user to remove one member from the team.

Results

- All users were able to successfully create a team of 5 members at an average of 5 minutes. Users who did not have their team member's email addresses readily available expressed frustration. Administrators will need a more efficient way to enter or upload this data.

- All users successfully assigned a course to their team. It took an average of 2 minutes to search for a specific course to assign.

- All users were able to remove one person from their team with ease.

Final Designs

After launch, team leaders were asked to take a survey about their experience. Over 80% users noted ease of managing their teams by facilitating assignments, viewing progress data, and generating reports.
